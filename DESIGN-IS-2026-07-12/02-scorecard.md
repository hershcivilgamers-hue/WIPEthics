# 02 — Scorecard (0–3 each, worst-instance scoring)

1.  Innovative — **2/3.** Refreshes the in-fiction document genre (classification bands, control
    furniture, redaction awareness) with restraint; not novel beyond the genre.
2.  Useful — **3/3.** One click → print-ready, redaction-aware paper; no decoy actions (2 screen-only buttons).
3.  Aesthetic — **1/3.** Letterhead wraps (screenshot); two title systems; red interviewer accent on the
    candidate sheet; metadata-before-issuer ordering. 3+ inconsistencies → 1.
4.  Understandable — **3/3.** Every document names itself in band + title; addressee/issuer/date labelled.
5.  Unobtrusive — **2/3.** Watermark and chrome quiet, but the control table competes with the issuer at the head.
6.  Honest — **3/3.** Labels map to behaviour; interviewer-only material provably absent from the candidate sheet.
7.  Long-lasting — **3/3.** Paper conventions (serif, rules, seals) age well; no trend markers.
8.  Thorough — **2/3.** Print rules, empty-states ("No answer was recorded"), page-break guards present;
    letterhead wrap + caveat wrap are rough edges.
9.  Environmentally friendly — **3/3.** ~85KB self-contained, no requests, no motion.
10. As little design as possible — **2/3.** Removable: duplicate title system, spacer div, near-duplicate tables.

**Total: 24/30. No zeros.**
